The Problem

Streets suffer.
Workers struggle.
Waste piles up.

India's rapid urbanisation has outpaced its waste-management infrastructure. Citizens have no reliable way to report garbage, and the workers who collect it lack safety, fair wages, or a future.

  • 🗑️ Large garbage piles block roads and cause traffic congestion
  • 🤢 Odour and vermin increase disease risk in communities
  • 📞 No reliable channel between citizens and waste services
  • ♻️ Valuable recyclable materials are lost to unsorted landfills
  • 👷 Waste workers lack safety equipment, fair pay, or career paths

The Solution

From report to resource —
in four steps.

STEP 01
📍
Citizens Report

Anyone can report a garbage pile with a photo, GPS location, and description. Reports appear on a live public map. Others can upvote to raise priority.

STEP 02
🔍
AI Verification

GPS, timestamps, and crowdsourced confirmation reduce false reports. AI estimates pile size and severity from photos to help dispatch teams efficiently.

STEP 03
🧹
Trained Teams Clean Up

Locally recruited and trained workers — equipped with safety gear and fair wages — are dispatched to high-priority sites using optimised routes.

STEP 04
🔄
Waste Becomes Value

Sorted materials go to recycling partners. Organic waste becomes compost sold to farmers. Nothing is wasted — the loop is truly circular.
